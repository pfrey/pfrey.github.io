PIXEL/code Style Guide

Logo

Logo Guidelines

  • Primary Logo:
    • Always use the full PIXEL/code logo with the slogan ("design • develop • deliver") when possible.
    • Logo works best on light or neutral backgrounds.
    • Maintain clear space around the logo — at least the height of the "P" on all sides.
    • Never stretch, rotate, recolor, or add effects to the logo.
  • Minimum Size:
    • Digital: 150px wide minimum
    • Print: 1 inch wide minimum

Alternate Logos

Alternate Primary Logo

  • Logo without the slogan for smaller spaces (e.g. social media icons, favicon).

Square Logo

  • Use for social media avatars, mobile icons, and anywhere a square format fits better.

Favicon

  • Use for browser tabs and mobile bookmarks.

Typography

Primary Fonts

  • PIXEL: Retro Pixelated Font (e.g. Press Start 2P, Pixel Emulator, or custom pixel fonts)
  • code: Monospace Font (e.g. Fira Code, Courier New)
  • Slogan: Monospace or simple Sans Serif (like Arial, Open Sans)

Secondary Fonts

  • PIXEL: Retro Pixelated Font
    (e.g. Press Start 2P, Pixel Emulator, or custom pixel fonts)
  • code: Monospace Font
    (e.g. Fira Code, Courier New)
  • Slogan: Monospace (e.g. Courier New) or simple Sans Serif (e.g. Arial, Open Sans)

Typography Guidelines

  • PIXEL text always UPPERCASE
  • /code text lowercase for stylistic balance
  • Keep clear hierarchy: headline > subhead > body text
  • Maintain lots of white space for a clean, breathable look

General Usage Guidelines

  • Keep designs clean and minimal.
  • Maintain consistency between digital and print materials.
  • Use retro and code aesthetics thoughtfully — don't overcrowd designs.
  • Prioritize clarity and responsiveness in web designs.
